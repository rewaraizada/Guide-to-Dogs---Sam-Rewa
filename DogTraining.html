DOG TRAINING



Whether you have a Boxer, Chihuahua, German Shepherd, etc. below are the commands that every dog should learn during the training process.

The dog’s ability to learn in training depends on the type.

It is recommended that you do this daily and for 10-15 minutes per session to make it most effective.


First, in HOUSE TRAINING make sure to limit the space a puppy has to the house and gradually start letting them in, in more spots. Making a routine of when the puppy eats, sleeps, plays, and goes outside will help in the early stages. While they are young they might accidently break one of the rules so it is important not to punish them but reward them with treats when they do something right.

Second, the dog should know the COME command. This can be learnt with having a leash on the puppy and staying away from the puppy and have a treat as a lure when the puppy comes towards you, use a clicker or say “yes”, and reward it with a treat.

Third, teach your dog the STAY command. Many trainers will tell you about the 3 D’s: Distance, Duration, and Distraction. Ultimately, the puppy should be able to sit and you should be able to sit as you start forming distance as the puppy gets the idea of the command. Using hand gestures is a great way for the dog to remember.

Fourth, teach your dog the LEAVE IT command. A recommended way for a dog to start learning this is by showing your dog a treat, the placing it under your foot, and reward them when they start to back off after sniffing around and repeating this practice.

Fifth, one of the most useful commands is the SIT command. One way to teach the sit command is to grab a treat and put it on the puppy’s nose and gradually move it to the back of her/his head which will make its butt go down and once that happens you can reward it with the treat.

Sixth, a command following the sit command is DOWN. One way to teach the command is holding the treat near his/her face and eventually moving it down and once their body hits the ground you can reward his/her with the treat and repeat this command.

Seventh, is the SETTLE command. This command helps them calm down when they are in a fear or anxious state. To teach this command you can leash the dog and step on the leash when they are in an excited state and once they settle down and know they can’t go anywhere you can say yes or click and give him/her a treat.
