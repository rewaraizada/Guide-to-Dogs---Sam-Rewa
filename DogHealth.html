DOG HEALTH



In order for your puppy to stay strong and healthy, it is important to keep regular visits with your veterinarian.

Dog health issues depend on your dog and the type as well.

Below is the most common dog health issues to which you should all speak to your vet about.




Name Information
Arthritis It is important to look for this issue in your dog as it gets older, you will notice your dog not being as active and having a harder time getting up. Unfortunately, there is no cure but you can take on actions to make it easier.
Worms You will notice weight loss, a swollen belly, diarrhea, and less energy. There are medications to fix worms.
Allergies There are many types of allergies but lack of energy, cough, or anything out of the ordinary will indicate some type of allergy. Changing the type of shampoo or food can be taken under consideration and it could be due to seasonal changes which medications help.
Kennel Cough This is a respiratory infection that transmit between dogs. There might be lethargy, coughing, leaky nose or eyes, and less of an appetite. Medications and vaccines help, and keeping your dog away from other dogs.
Vomiting Make sure to take note on how what events lead up to the vomiting. If vomiting is frequent the best option is to take your dog to the vet.
Obesity Keeping note of your dog’s weight over time is important and keeping a good balance of diet and exercise.
Cancer If your dog has unusual odors/smells, lumps, weight loss, and changes in behavior this may indicate cancer. See your vet as soon as possible and surgery is possible can be fixed if it is noticed earlier.
Rabies This disease is less seen due to vaccinations nowadays, and watching your dog’s activity and getting shots on time is important to prevent this.
